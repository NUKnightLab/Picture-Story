Back to our Roots

by Sam Schumacher

Photographs by Sam Schumacher

Young Greeks dress in traditional garb to represent their weekend cultural-education school and church.

While normally a busy, industrious locale, the corner of Halsted and Randolph street was especially alight April 17th as parade goers awaited the start of Chicago’s annual Hellenic Heritage Parade.

Following weeks of cold, overcast weather, the residents of Greektown turned out in droves in celebration of the 195th anniversary of Greece’s declaration of independence. Families, 20-somethings, and Orthodox priests alike lined the curbs of Halsted, waiting in the sunshine for the parade to begin.

An annual tradition for the neighborhood, the parade drew hundreds, and thousands of “Zito Hallas” and “Zito Chicago” chants peppered the air west of the Chicago River. The spirited chants translate roughly to, “Hail Greece,” and “Hail Chicago,”.

The parade stands both as an entertainment attraction to residents of the area as well as a deeper celebration of Hellenic culture. Chicago dignitaries and Greek cultural groups alike marched in April 17th’s parade, representing organizations from throughout the Chicagoland area as well as Greece. Festivities even poured into area restaurants such as Artopolis, that had a view of the street.

As the day grew longer, parade goers dispersed throughout neighboring streets, stopping at local favorites such as Mr. Greek Gyros and The Parthenon. Eating seemed almost to be a main facet of the day. Food, fun, and facial hair were plentiful on both side of Halsted.
