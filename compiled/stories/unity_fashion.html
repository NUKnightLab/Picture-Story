Rock the Runway

UNITY celebrates "renewal" for its ninth annual charity fashion show.

by NATALIE ESCOBAR

Photographs by NATALIE ESCOBAR

Audience members gaze up at the final succession of models to parade down the runway. Different groups of models' families and friends cheered extra-loudly when their respective model passed by.

To runway observers, UNITY's show seemed simple enough: models parading down a runway in different clothes, strutting slightly differently each time they emerged from the curtains at the start of the catwalk.

But those curtains partitioned off the performance from the preparation and process. In the narrow spaces between clothing racks, models ripped the ensembles off their backs in seconds, only to throw on new garb right before they took the stage. Assistants sped past each other, handing each other garments and trying to not knock anyone over. Fashion photographers snapped pictures of the different get-ups – and then themselves.

The Unity Charity has grown a lot in the nine years since its initial show, with 24 models and corporate partners that provided clothing to be worn down the runway. Staged at the Hilton Orrington Hotel, hundreds of audience members packed into the ballroom to watch their fellow Northwestern students walk the runway.

The year's theme, "Renewal," celebrated the growing conversations about environmental sustainability that have recently taken hold of the fashion world. The clothing choices evoked that theme, with lots of vintage pieces recycled from the 90s.

For the third year in a row, UNITY donated all of the proceeds of the event to NoStigmas, an organization based in Chicago that advocates for mental health destigmatization.
