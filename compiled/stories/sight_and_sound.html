Sight and Sound

Bienen Contemporary Music Ensemble performs new pieces by David Lang.

by LOUIS LU

Photographs by LOUIS LU

Bienen Contemporary Music Ensemble performs in Galvin Hall

Enjoyment of both music and a breathtaking view of Chicago.

Bienen Contemporary Music Ensemble delivered a stunning performance in the Mary B. Galvin Recital Hall on Sunday April 17, 2016, backed by a view of Chicago’s skyline. The program featured a new composition by David Lang, the 2008 Pulitzer Prize winner.

Lang is one of America’s most performed composers. His works juxtapose classical traditions in musical art and his own interpretation of contemporary music. Many of his works resist categorization and seek to expand the definition of music. Before the program on Sunday, Lang spoke about his unique compositional style and his new piece “a house” which will be premiered in the program.

As a visiting artist, Lang worked with Donald Nally from Bienen School of Music and produced the performance in the new Mary B. Galvin Recital Hall of the Ryan Center for the Musical Arts.

I wondered if I could mirror in my piece the building of a building, if I could lay out my bricks and my mortar, and then painstakingly assemble them into chambers, filled with riches.

David Lang

In his notes during the program, Lang said that he started to think about the piece as a wish for the future of music. With the soothing sound of the ensemble and a breathtaking view of the recital hall, the audience received an enjoyment of both sight and sound.
