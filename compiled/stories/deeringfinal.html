Deering After Dark

The Library Gets "Lit"erary

by ALEX FURUYA

Photographs by ALEX FURUYA

Students enjoy putting in the makeshift mini golf course in the Art Collection of Deering Library.

As part of Love your Library week, Deering Library hosts its annual quirky event full of music, games and food.

For once, there is a commotion in Deering Library. On April 14, the Northwestern library hosted an event called “Deering After Dark,” an event where students could come to the Deering Library for free and enjoy a night of activities and food.

As an event that is part of Love Your Library Week, Deering Library was open from 10 p.m. to 2 a.m. Students were invited to take part of the various activities at the library. In the Art Collection room, the librarians set up a massive mini golf course in the nooks and crannies of the room. Students could putt within the narrow aisles of books. In the other rooms of the second level of Deering were video games and board games.

Not only did the students enjoy the event, the library sta also joined to meet and get to know the students. It was an interesting sight, seeing librarians eating popcorn and drinking soda next to valuable books.

"Deering After Dark" was meant to introduce students to Deering Library and to let them meet the staff who work there. This event allowed students to visit Deering Library in an informal and casual setting. Some students were surprised to see the parts of the library they have never seen before.
